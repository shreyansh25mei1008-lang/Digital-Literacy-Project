Stay Safe Online: A Guide for Students
Being a "Digital Ambassador" means staying one step ahead of scammers. Use this checklist to protect your data and your money:
1. The Golden Rule of UPI: Always remember that you never need to enter your UPI PIN or scan a QR code to receive money. If someone asks you to "authorize" a payment coming into your account, it is a scam.
2. Verify the Source: Before clicking any links in an SMS or WhatsApp message (like those claiming your electricity will be cut off or you’ve won a lucky draw), check the official app or website instead.
3. Use Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Turn on 2-factor authentication for your Gmail, Instagram, and LinkedIn accounts. This adds a "second lock" that hackers can't bypass with just your password.
4. Secure Your UPI App: Set a strong biometric lock (fingerprint or Face ID) or a separate pattern for your payment apps like GPay, PhonePe, or Paytm to prevent unauthorized access if your phone is misplaced.
5. Check for "HTTPS": Never enter personal information or login credentials on a website unless the URL starts with https:// and has a small padlock icon in the browser bar.
6. Public Wi-Fi Hygiene: Avoid logging into your bank account or making purchases while connected to free public Wi-Fi at cafes or railway stations, as these networks are often unencrypted.
7. Privacy Settings Review: Once every few months, check your social media privacy settings. Ensure your phone number and email address are not visible to the public.
8. Vanish the "Too Good to Be True" Offers: If an unknown person on a marketplace offers to pay the full price of an item without seeing it first, stop the conversation immediately.
